In the fast-paced realm of artificial intelligence (AI), building applications with foundation models is transforming the landscape, making AI accessible to everyone. Chip Huyen's book "AI Engineering" is a comprehensive guide that demystifies this journey. The book starts by setting the stage for understanding what AI engineering entails and comparing it to traditional machine learning engineering.

Foundation models have enabled countless new AI use cases, reducing barriers to entry significantly. This transition has shifted AI from being an arcane field into a powerful development tool accessible even to those without prior experience in AI. Chip Huyen's expertise shines through as she explains the process of building applications with readily available foundation models, covering everything from the foundational aspects of AI engineering.

She delves into practical frameworks and approaches for developing AI applications efficiently, discussing model adaptation techniques such as prompt engineering, RAG (Revised Annotation Generation), fine-tuning, agents, and dataset management. The book also explores bottlenecks in latency and cost when working with foundation models to provide solutions for overcoming these challenges.

The AI Engineering Stack consists of three layers: foundational knowledge, practical implementation steps, and a guide on how to evaluate AI systems effectively. The author offers valuable insights into the evolution from large language models to foundation models and discusses their applications across various fields such as image production, writing, education, conversational bots, information aggregation, data organization, workflow automation, and planning.

For evaluating AI systems, the book covers key evaluation criteria for different domains, including domain-specific capability, generation capability, instruction-following capability, cost and latency. It guides readers through the process of selecting models effectively, creating a clear evaluation pipeline, and evaluating all components in a system to ensure comprehensive results.

In-depth techniques like prompt engineering, RAG (Revised Annotation Generation), fine-tuning, and dataset management are presented with practical tips and best practices that can help developers navigate these technical complexities. The book concludes by discussing the challenges of evaluating AI systems comprehensively and offers solutions for improving accuracy without resorting to expensive fine-tuning.

For readers interested in building robust and scalable AI systems, "AI Engineering" serves as an essential guide from novice to expert level. It bridges the collaboration gap between cross-functional teams, ensuring that everyone involved is on the same page in their AI development efforts. The book's comprehensive coverage makes it a must-read for anyone looking to build real-world applications that can scale successfully.
